Capitalism Plants 2024-2025
Project explores the commodification of plants. Producers “beautify” plants in the name of profit by coating them with paint, with little regard for their survival. The paint impairs or entirely blocks photosynthesis, leading to the plant’s gradual weakening or even death. Succulents are the most frequent victims of such practices – their structure allows painted specimens to appear “healthy” for a long time before visibly deteriorating. Succulents can be propagated from individual leaves – a method that often becomes one of the few possible ways to rescue painted plants. The act of separating the plant into single leaves references this technique. Manipulation of greenery, however, extends beyond its physical form. Online, one can come across listings for entirely fabricated plant images, revealing how easily an image can appear trustworthy – and how fragile our trust in it can be.
